📐 Body Measurements

Accurate measurements are the foundation of a well-fitting garment. Take them wearing form-fitting undergarments, standing naturally, with a helper if possible.

💡 Golden rule: keep the tape measure snug but not tight — you should be able to slip one finger underneath it.

What You Need

📏 Flexible tape measure
🖊️ Pen and paper (or phone)
🪞 Full-length mirror
🧍 A helper (ideal)
👙 Form-fitting undergarments

Measurement Points at a Glance

1
Bust — fullest part of the chest
2
Waist — narrowest part of the torso
3
Hip — fullest part of the hips, ~20cm below waist
4
Shoulder width — across the back, shoulder to shoulder
5
Back length — nape of neck to natural waist
6
Inseam — crotch to floor
7
Neck — circumference around the base of the neck

How to Take Each Measurement

1 Bust / Chest
Wrap the tape around the fullest part of the chest, keeping it parallel to the floor. Breathe normally — don't expand or contract.
For tops, jackets, and dresses.
2 Waist
Find your natural waist by bending side to side — it's the crease that forms. Measure around that point, keeping the tape comfortably snug.
Typically 2–3 cm above the belly button.
3 Hip
Stand with feet together. Measure around the fullest part of the hips and seat, usually about 18–23 cm below the natural waist.
Note how far below the waist your hip fullness falls — useful for skirt and trouser fitting.
4 Shoulder Width
Measure across the upper back from the point of one shoulder to the other (where the shoulder seam would sit), in a straight line.
Best taken with a helper for accuracy.
5 Back Length
From the most prominent vertebra at the nape of the neck, straight down the center back to the natural waist.
Used for bodice blocks and dress patterns.
6 Inseam
Stand straight. Measure from the crotch seam (inner thigh) down to the floor. Easier with a helper or by measuring a well-fitting pair of trousers.
For trousers and jeans. Note if you want ankle, midi, or full-length.
7 Neck Circumference
Wrap the tape around the base of the neck. Keep one finger between the tape and neck for ease.
Used for collars and necklines.
↕ Bust Height
From the shoulder (at the neckline) straight down to the bust point (nipple level). Taken vertically down the front of the body.
Used for dart placement in bodice patterns.
↔ Bust Span
The horizontal distance between the two bust points (nipple to nipple).
Used alongside bust height for accurate dart manipulation in Clo3D.
↕ Outseam
From the natural waist, down the outer side of the leg to the floor.
For trousers and skirts. Compare to inseam to determine crotch depth.
